## 5. Neutrino Oscillations as Topological Interference

### 5.1 The Oscillation Phenomenon

Neutrinos produced in a specific **flavor eigenstate** (νₑ, νμ, ντ) propagate as **mass eigenstates** (ν₁, ν₂, ν₃) with different energies. After traveling distance L, the quantum phases accumulate differently:

$$\phi_i = \frac{m_i^2 L}{2E}$$

The interference between mass eigenstates causes the probability of detecting a different flavor to oscillate:

$$P(\nu_\alpha \to \nu_\beta) = \sum_{i,j} U_{\alpha i}^* U_{\beta i} U_{\alpha j} U_{\beta j}^* e^{-i\Delta m_{ij}^2 L / 2E}$$

where $U$ is the **PMNS mixing matrix** (neutrino analog of CKM matrix).

### 5.2 Flux Medium Interpretation

In the flux medium picture, neutrino oscillations arise from **topological beating**:

- Each mass eigenstate (ν₁, ν₂, ν₃) has a slightly different fractional winding (n₁, n₂, n₃)
- Different windings → different phase velocities through the flux medium
- Quantum superposition of these states creates **interference patterns**
- Observed as flavor oscillations (νₑ ↔ νμ ↔ ντ)

**Analogy**: Like acoustic beats from two slightly detuned tuning forks, the fractional winding differences create a slow "beat frequency" in flavor space.

### 5.3 Why Mixing is Large (Unlike Quarks)

Neutrinos exhibit **large mixing angles** (θ₁₂ ≈ 33°, θ₂₃ ≈ 45°, θ₁₃ ≈ 8.5°), unlike quarks which have small mixing.

**Flux medium explanation**:
- **Quarks**: Confined by strong QCD potential → winding states locked in place → small mixing

When winding number approaches zero (n → 0), the **topological identity becomes fuzzy**—different unwinding modes overlap significantly, creating large mixing angles naturally.

In the early hot Big Bang (T > 1 MeV), neutrinos were in thermal equilibrium. As the universe cooled, they decoupled at **T ~ 1 MeV** (t ~ 1 second), creating the **Cosmic Neutrino Background** (CNB).

**Fractional winding interpretation**:
- At high temperatures, thermal energy >> $m_\nu$ → neutrinos behave relativistically
- As universe cools, $T < m_\nu$ → neutrinos become non-relativistic (if massive)
- Current CNB temperature: $T_\nu \approx 1.95$ K ≈ 0.00017 eV

Since $m_\nu \sim 0.05$ eV >> $T_\nu$, relic neutrinos are **non-relativistic today**.

**Testable**: Future surveys (Euclid, DESI, Rubin LSST) will measure structure suppression to distinguish $\sum m_\nu = 0.06$ eV from zero.
